How to play Rock Paper Scissors

After a count of 3, Two Players throw a rock, paper, or scissors hand gesture:
  • Rock beats Scissors
  • Scissors beats Paper
  • Paper beats Rock

How Old is Rock Paper Scissors?

According to the book "Wuzazu", Rock Paper Scissors dates back to the Han dynasty (206 BCE - 220 CE). Although the modern version using "Rock, Paper, Scissors" signs emerged in Japan around the 17th century as "Jan-ken-pon."

When did Rock Paper Scissors Spread to the Rest of the World?

Rock Paper Scissors spread to the rest of the world in the earliest 20th century when Japan began to have incre with the West. One of the earliest recorded mentions of the game in the West was in a book "Korean Games: With Notes on the Corresponding Games of China and Japan" by Stewart Culin published in 1895. The book provides some insight into how the game might have been introduced to the west.
